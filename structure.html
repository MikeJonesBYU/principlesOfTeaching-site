Focus on Jesus Christ (12 skills)
Teach about Jesus Christ No Matter What You are Teaching
Emphasize the example of Jesus Christ
  • Help students connect what they are learning with how Christ exemplifies the principle.
  • Use pictures and videos of Jesus Christ to illustrate a gospel principle.
Teach about the titles, roles, and attributes of Jesus Christ
  • Create search questions to help students identify roles, titles, symbols, attributes, and characteristics of Jesus Christ.
  • Ask questions that allow students to identify roles Jesus Christ can have in their lives.
Look for symbols that testify of Jesus Christ
  • Ask questions that help students learn about Jesus Christ through symbols in the scriptures.
  • Help students have a sensory experience with scriptural objects that symbolize Jesus Christ.
Help Learners Come unto Jesus Christ
Help learners recognize the Lord’s love, power, and mercy in their lives
  • Ask questions that focus on connecting the Lord’s power, mercy, and influence to the truths being taught.
  • Give invitations that help learners recognize the Lord’s love, power, and mercy in their own experiences.
Help learners strengthen their relationship with Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ
  • Statements that help students know and feel the love of Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ.
  • Ask questions that help students find examples of Heavenly Father’s love in the scripture passage.
Help learners intentionally strive to be more like Jesus Christ
  • Observe Christlike attributes in students and share what you’ve noticed in ways that inspire them to continue to be like Him.
  • Invite students to look for ways to follow the example of Jesus Christ in their personal lives.
Love Those You Teach (11 skills)
See learners the way God sees them
  • Study recent prophetic messages to understand how Heavenly Father sees young people.
  • “Think celestial” about your students to help you see them as God does.
Seek to know them—understand their circumstances, needs, and strengths
  • Observe and ask about students’ interests.
  • Pause, reflect, and answer questions we ask ourselves that invite a Christlike spirit of discernment, love, and empathy in our interactions.
  • Seek to clarify and understand the real intent of students’ questions, feelings, and beliefs.
Pray for them by name
  • Pray and ask how you can help your students and follow the promptings from the Holy Ghost.
  • Invite students to pray for other students.
Create a safe environment where all are respected and know their contributions are valued
  • Communicate that you value students before they comment or as they raise their hand to comment.
  • Communicate that students are not only welcome but needed.
Find appropriate ways to express your love
  • Send a message to a student’s parent about something positive you have noticed about their child.
  • Testify of God’s love when it is hard for you to feel or express love to those you teach.
Teach By the Spirit (10 skills)
Prepare yourself spiritually
  • Use the document “Improving as a Christlike Teacher—A Personal Evaluation” to invite the Holy Ghost to help you refine your preparations to teach.
  • Ask questions to assess your own experience and testimony with Jesus Christ and the doctrine and principles in the lesson.
Always be ready to respond to spiritual promptings about the needs of learners
  • Ask a question to assess learning before moving on in the lesson.
  • Listen to and observe students to ask follow-up questions.
Create settings and opportunities for learners to be taught by the Holy Ghost
  • Create student self-evaluations about a doctrine, truth, or principle.
  • Use sacred music. Invite students to identify lines and phrases in sacred music that connect with the truths they are learning.
Help learners seek, recognize, and act on personal revelation
  • Before responding to a student’s question or comment, pause and think, “What can I ask them?” or “What can I invite them to do?”
  • Share statements that help students recognize when the Holy Ghost is performing His role or function.
Bear testimony often and invite learners to share their feelings, experiences, and testimonies
  • Create prompts that help students verbalize their feelings, experiences, and testimony.
  • Testify more frequently and more powerfully of Jesus Christ.
Teach the Doctrine (10 skills)
Learn the doctrine of Jesus Christ for yourself
  • Create open-ended search questions that help learners discover gospel doctrine and principles for themselves and do not lead students to a specific response.
  • Search the scriptures and words of the prophets for deeper understanding.
Teach from the scriptures and the words of latter-day prophets
  • Prepare invitations that help students connect truths found in the scriptures to what living prophets are saying.
  • Express your love for and testimony of prophets as their words are shared in class.
Help learners seek, recognize, and understand truths in the scriptures
  • Create student self-evaluations that help them recognize their current understanding and abilities about scripture study skills.
  • Consider assessment questions to carefully choose media, personal stories, and object lessons.
Focus on truths that lead to conversion and build faith in Jesus Christ
  • Ask questions that help students identify and state converting principles.
  • Respond to questions in a way that avoids speculation and nondoctrinal personal ideas.
Help learners find personal relevance in the doctrine of Jesus Christ
  • Prepare invitations and prompts that help students find personal relevance to a scripture block.
  • Start a learning activity by inviting students to ponder a personal circumstance.
Invite Diligent Learning (10 skills)
Help learners take responsibility for their learning
  • Resist the tendency to respond to every comment and question and invite the class to respond.
  • Focus on what the learner can do in class that the teacher typically does.
Encourage learners to come to know the Savior by studying the gospel daily—study goal
  • Assist students in creating a daily scripture study goal.
  • Develop and ask questions that help learners connect what they have learned in their personal and family scripture study to the classroom experience.
Invite learners to prepare to learn
  • Create invitations that help learners prepare for the next learning experience.
  • Create a meaningful invitation connected to the lesson outcome to be used at the beginning of each lesson.
Encourage learners to share the truths they are learning
  • Help students create or begin gospel conversations.
  • Prepare invitations for students to share with each other what they are learning.
Invite learners to live what they are learning
  • Plan to follow up on invitations given in a previous class and invite learners to share their experiences living what they learned.
  • Ask search questions that help learners consider who God is and blessings He offers them.
